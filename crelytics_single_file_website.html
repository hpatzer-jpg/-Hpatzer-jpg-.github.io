Commercial real estate still runs on outdated decisions

Owners and investors are dealing with rising vacancies, refinancing pressure, and market uncertainty. But many leasing and underwriting decisions are still made using stale comps, manual spreadsheets, and gut instinct.

Rent pricing is often reactive

Teams rely on broker opinions and historical comps instead of dynamic market signals, causing either lost revenue or unnecessary vacancy.

Tenant risk is hard to quantify

Operators often lack a clear way to estimate renewal risk, churn, or default probability before lease decisions are made.

Underwriting is too manual

Deals can still take hours of spreadsheet work, with assumptions scattered across tabs and little connection to real-time market conditions.

Teams miss early warning signs

By the time risks show up in occupancy or collections, the best options to improve asset performance are already gone.
